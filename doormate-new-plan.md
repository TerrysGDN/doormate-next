## 1. WHAT THE OLD SITE DID THAT WORKED — CARRY EVERY ONE OF THESE TO THE NEW SITE

### Sales Mechanics That Convert
- **BUY NOW buttons on every product with "From £X.XX" prices** — present on all old product pages. This is the format. Never deviate.
- **★★★★★ star ratings on product tiles** — even without a review count, presence of stars helps. Keep on every product tile.
- **POPULAR and NEW badges** — on shop products, these are conversion tools. They tell customers what sells and what's fresh. Must carry to new site shop/accessories pages.
- **Sizing formulas with worked examples** — the Gainesville page ("760mm opening → 840mm door → 1700mm track") is the single most useful content on the old site. Customers always ask this. These formulas remove the #1 barrier to buying. Every hardware product page needs one.
- **Spec request process** — "every order followed up with a spec request sheet" is something no Chinese importer does. It must be stated on every barn door hardware page. It is the proof that DoorMate actually manufactures.
- **Anti-imitation copy** — "Don't Accept Cheap Imitations" on the homepage is Terry's sales voice at its sharpest. Specific, believable, rooted in real problems (poor fixing points, undersized tracks, noisy rollers). Carry it straight to the new site.
- **12-year Rocket warranty** — the strongest warranty claim in the pocket door kit category. Not prominent enough on the old site. Must be headline prominent on new site Rocket page and on the pocket door kits landing page.
- **5-step installation enquiry form** — qualifies the customer before they call. Currently only on Pocket Door Kits nav. New site must link it from every relevant product page.
- **Free site survey CTA** — on the services page. Low-barrier first contact. Keep.
- **FAQ pages** — 20 barn door questions and 11 pocket door questions exist on the old site. These are exactly the questions Google ranks on and AI assistants quote. Must carry all 31 questions to the new site, as visible text with FAQPage structured data.
- **Gallery — 150+ named installation images** — volume matters. Named alt text on every image is a small but real SEO win. All images carry. The design names (V-Brace, Z-Brace, Ledge & Brace, H X Brace, Union Jack, Mirror, Steel Strap) are all visible in the gallery — new site should surface these on the handmade doors page.

### Trust Signals That Work
- **"UK Manufactured"** sub-label on barn door hardware product tiles — one line, but it differentiates from every Chinese import competitor.
- **Channel 4 credential** — one of the strongest trust signals for a UK consumer. Currently buried on a thin "As Seen On TV" page with no images. New site gives it a proper page with episode reference (S8, E3), imagery, and embedded clip if available.

- **Houzz reviews** — the old site has a Houzz widget embedded on the homepage. The new site already has this in the "Who We Are" section. Keep it prominent.
- **Production updates with photos during manufacture** — mentioned on the About Us page. This is a genuine service differentiator that no competitor offers. Must appear on barn door hardware and handmade doors product pages.
- **Workshop address** (Rear of 81 Richard Terrace, Cardiff, CF24 1RW) — a physical address builds trust. Must be in footer and Contact page.

- **"Built for real doors, real walls and real homes"** — locked tagline, already on new site. Good.

## 3. WHAT TODAY'S TECHNOLOGY ADDS THAT THE OLD SITE NEVER HAD

### Google and AI Visibility
- **Schema.org JSON-LD structured data** on every page. The old site has none. This is why Google doesn't show rich snippets (star ratings, prices, FAQs in search results) for DoorMate. Every page on the new site needs: Organisation + LocalBusiness (in layout), Product + AggregateRating (on every product page), FAQPage (on FAQ content), BreadcrumbList (on category and product pages).
- **llms.txt file at root** — a curated summary of the site for AI assistants (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini). Means DoorMate appears when someone asks an AI "where can I buy a pocket door kit UK". Takes 20 minutes to build. High value.
- **Direct FAQ answers as visible text** — AI assistants quote verbatim from pages. "What headroom do I need for the Gainesville?" answered in plain English on the page = DoorMate gets cited in AI responses.
- **Open Graph meta tags** on every page — controls how the site appears when someone shares a link on Facebook, WhatsApp, LinkedIn. Old site has none.
- **Proper H1/H2/H3 hierarchy** — old site is flat. New site uses Next.js with semantic structure. Google and AI assistants read this hierarchy to understand what a page is about.

### Conversion Machinery
- **Chatbot in every product page corner** — the DoorMate chatbot (doormate-chat-widget.html, knowledge base in Google Sheet, API key on Notepad) is built and works. It is not live on any page yet. This is the after-hours sales person. Wiring it in is a code task, not a build-from-scratch task.
- **Quote calculators** — currently half-built. Even a v1 (2 questions: opening size, door type → recommended product + price) is better than nothing. Captures the lead whether the customer buys or not.
- **Cross-sell blocks ("Frequently Bought Together")** on every product page — pull handles, floor guides, locks, soft close units against barn door hardware. Pocket Door Privacy Lock (£29.94) against every pocket door kit. Pure margin on every transaction.
- **Stripe checkout** with Card, PayPal, Apple Pay, Google Pay, Klarna/Clearpay — Terry has an existing Stripe account. Old site uses WebFactory checkout. New site opens up all payment methods.
- **Dedicated /reviews page** — collecting Google (4.8★), Trustpilot (4.5★), Houzz (5.0★) in one place. Currently split across three platforms with no single destination.
